## 🟢 Nvidia GTC 2026: Jensen Sees $1 Trillion, Unveils Vera Rubin, and Brings Olaf to Life

Jensen Huang delivered a nearly 3-hour keynote at GTC in San Jose, announcing that Nvidia expects $1 trillion in purchase orders through 2027 across Blackwell and Vera Rubin platforms. The Vera Rubin supercomputer — 7 chips, 1.3 million components, 10x performance per watt — ships later this year. He also unveiled the Groq 3 LPU from Nvidia's $20B acquisition, which boosts inference performance by 35x per watt. And yes, Disney brought an AI-powered Olaf robot on stage. The snowman walked.

## 🟡 Nvidia Launches NemoClaw — An Agentic AI Platform Inspired by OpenClaw

In a direct nod to the viral OpenClaw project, Nvidia announced NemoClaw — an open-source stack for running always-on AI agents. It includes the Nvidia OpenShell runtime, part of their Agent Toolkit, and supports open-source models like Nemotron. Jensen praised OpenClaw's community during the keynote.
