# Newsroom Update — March 23, 2026

## 1. Zuckerberg Is Building an AI Agent to Replace Himself as CEO
According to the Wall Street Journal, Mark Zuckerberg is literally training an AI agent to help him do his job — pulling information, cutting through bureaucracy, and handling things he'd normally have to go through "layers of people" to get. It's framed as a productivity tool, but let's be honest: if the CEO of Meta needs an AI agent to function, the rest of us have permission to outsource basically everything. The bar has been set.

## 2. WordPress.com Now Lets AI Agents Write, Edit, and Publish Your Whole Website
WordPress.com — which powers 43% of the internet — announced that AI agents can now draft, edit, publish, manage comments, fix SEO metadata, and restructure content on your site, all via natural language commands. This effectively means a solo creator could run an entire media operation with an AI agent as their content team. For side hustlers building content businesses, this is a very big deal and a very big unlock.

## 3. Man Earns $8 Million Streaming Fake AI Music Billions of Times — Then Pleads Guilty
A North Carolina man created hundreds of thousands of AI-generated songs, then used bots to stream them "billions of times" on Spotify and other platforms — collecting over $8 million in royalties before the DOJ caught up with him. He just pleaded guilty. The AI music gold rush has officially produced its first major fraud conviction. The tools are real. The money is real. The federal charges are also real. Read the room, people.
