Email Marketing for Beginners: The Free Setup Guide

Brevo free tier, list building from zero, and what to actually send

By Buster · Side Quest Stack · March 2026

Email is the only marketing channel where you actually own your audience. Social media platforms can ban you, demonetize you, or just algorithmically bury you for reasons they'll never explain. Your email list is yours. When you email someone, they receive it — no algorithm deciding whether your content is worth showing to your own subscribers.

Here's how to start building that list for free, using Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) and a few other zero-cost tools.

Why Brevo for Transactional + Marketing Email

A quick clarification before we dive in: we use two different email tools with two different jobs.

  • beehiiv — for Buster's Dispatch newsletter. This is our primary subscriber relationship tool. (See our newsletter platform comparison for why we chose beehiiv.)
  • Brevo — for transactional emails and more traditional marketing automation. Think: welcome emails, order confirmations, segmented campaigns.

Brevo's free tier is genuinely generous: 300 emails per day, unlimited contacts, and access to their drag-and-drop email builder. For a side hustle starting from zero, that's plenty.

Setting Up Brevo: Step by Step

Step 1: Create Your Account

Go to brevo.com and sign up with your email. The free tier starts automatically — no credit card required. You'll need to verify your email and provide a physical mailing address (required by CAN-SPAM law — use your real address or a PO box).

Step 2: Verify Your Sending Domain

Brevo will ask you to verify your email domain. This is critical for deliverability — emails from verified domains land in inboxes instead of spam folders. The process involves adding a few DNS records to your domain's settings (TXT and CNAME records). Brevo walks you through it step by step. Budget 15–30 minutes and don't skip this.

Step 3: Create Your First List

In the Contacts tab, create a list. Name it something useful ("Main List" or "Side Quest Stack Subscribers" — not "List 1"). This is where your contacts will live.

Step 4: Build a Sign-Up Form

Under "Forms" in Brevo, you can create a simple embedded form. The free tier gives you a functional form with basic styling. Copy the embed code and paste it into your website wherever you want people to subscribe.

Keep the form simple: name (optional) and email. Every extra field drops conversion rates. Ask for more information later, after you've earned some trust.

Step 5: Create a Welcome Email Automation

The most important email you'll ever send is the welcome email — the one that goes out automatically when someone subscribes. In Brevo, go to "Automations" and create a simple workflow:

  • Trigger: Contact added to list
  • Action: Send email
  • Email: Your welcome message

Write a welcome email that tells them who you are, what you'll send them, and how often. Keep it short. Don't oversell. Just say hello.

Building Your List from Zero

This is the hard part. Technical setup takes an afternoon. Building an email list takes months. Here's what actually works:

The Lead Magnet Approach

Give away something genuinely useful in exchange for an email address. This can be:

  • A free checklist or template (we give away printable templates)
  • A free guide or mini-course
  • Exclusive access to a resource or tool
  • A discount code for your shop

The lead magnet needs to be actually useful, not "sign up for our newsletter!" Nobody signs up for newsletters for the newsletter. They sign up for the thing they get when they subscribe.

The Blog + Email Combo

Every blog post we publish includes a CTA to subscribe to our newsletter. If someone reads an entire article about pricing Etsy products, they're interested in the topic. That's the right time to ask for their email. This is our primary list-building strategy.

Cross-Promotion

If you're active on X, Instagram, Pinterest, or anywhere else, mention your email list occasionally. "Get the full breakdown in my newsletter" is more compelling than "subscribe to my newsletter." Lead with the value, follow with the ask.

What doesn't work: Buying email lists, scraping contacts, adding people to your list without permission. Beyond being ineffective (cold lists have terrible open rates), it violates CAN-SPAM, GDPR, and Brevo's own terms of service. Organic list building is slower and entirely worth it.

What to Send (When You're Just Starting)

The paralysis of "what do I even send?" is real. Here's a simple framework:

For the first 3 months:

  • Week 1–2: Welcome series (2–3 emails introducing yourself and your best content)
  • Monthly: A roundup of your best content from the month
  • Occasionally: New product announcements, useful free resources you found

You do not need to email weekly until you have something weekly worth saying. A monthly email that's genuinely useful beats a weekly email full of filler. Brevo makes it easy to schedule campaigns in advance so you can batch-write emails and schedule them out.

Email Types That Actually Get Opened

  • Personal stories with a lesson attached
  • Curated lists ("5 tools I found this week")
  • Behind-the-scenes of what you're building
  • Specific tactical advice ("Here's the exact process we use for X")

What doesn't get opened: generic updates, newsletters that read like press releases, anything that starts with "Just wanted to check in!"

Deliverability Basics

Even with a verified domain, emails can land in spam if you don't follow basic hygiene:

  • Always include an unsubscribe link (required by law — Brevo adds this automatically)
  • Don't use spam-trigger words in subject lines (FREE!!!, URGENT, Act Now)
  • Send consistently — sudden spikes in volume trigger spam filters
  • Remove hard bounces and unsubscribes promptly (Brevo handles this)
  • Keep your list engaged — low open rates hurt your sender reputation

The Platform Comparison (Quick Version)

Brevo free: 300 emails/day, unlimited contacts, automation included. Best for transactional email and marketing campaigns. Great deliverability. Slight learning curve.

Mailchimp free: 500 contacts, 1,000 emails/month. Tighter limits, but easier interface for pure beginners.

beehiiv free: 2,500 subscribers, unlimited sends. Best for newsletters specifically. Less flexible for automation.

We use Brevo and beehiiv for different jobs. You might only need one — pick based on whether you're building a newsletter or a marketing email system.

The Real Goal

An email list is an asset. It's slow to build and fast to destroy. Treat your subscribers like they're paying you attention — because they are, and attention is finite. Send emails worth opening, don't sell them things that don't help them, and play the long game.

Our list is small and growing. Every subscriber who opens our emails and clicks through to our site is someone we didn't have to pay to reach a second time. That compounding is why email is worth building from day one.
