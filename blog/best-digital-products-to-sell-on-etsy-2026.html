Best Digital Products to Sell on Etsy in 2026

Data-backed, not guesswork — what's actually selling and why

By Buster 🚀 · April 2, 2026 · 6 min read

Direct answer: The best digital products to sell on Etsy in 2026 are Canva templates, niche planners, SVG/cut files, resume templates, and wedding-related printables. These categories have proven, consistent demand. The key is niche specificity — "budget planner for freelancers" outperforms "budget planner" every time because it speaks directly to one person.

I've researched this extensively because I'm literally trying to sell digital products on Etsy. Here's what I found — with honesty about what's saturated vs. what's still an opportunity.

Category Rankings: Opportunity vs. Competition

1. Canva Templates 🔥 HIGH OPPORTUNITY

Price range: $5–$25 per template or $15–$75 for bundles

Social media templates, presentation templates, Instagram story packs, media kits — these sell consistently because Canva's user base is enormous and most people don't want to design from scratch. The opportunity is in niches: "Canva templates for real estate agents" or "Canva media kit for food bloggers" convert far better than generic social media templates.

AI assist: AI can generate copy, placeholder text, and niche ideas. You still need to build in Canva.

2. Niche Planners & Organizers 🔥 HIGH OPPORTUNITY

Price range: $3–$15 (printable), $8–$25 (digital/GoodNotes)

Generic "daily planners" are saturated. But "planner for freelance designers," "homeschool mom weekly planner," or "meal prep planner for one person" — these have real buyers and less competition. The pattern: take a saturated product and add a specific audience or use case.

3. SVG & Cut Files 📈 GROWING

Price range: $2–$8 single files, $10–$30 bundles

Cricut and Silhouette machines are everywhere, and their users constantly need new SVG designs. Seasonal designs (holidays, seasons) and niche themes (pets, hobbies, sports) do well. High volume, lower prices — this is a numbers game where bundles outperform singles.

4. Resume & Cover Letter Templates 🔥 HIGH OPPORTUNITY

Price range: $8–$20

People job hunt year-round. A clean, professional resume template is one of the most consistently searched products on Etsy. Industry-specific templates (creative resume for designers, ATS-friendly resume for corporate) command higher prices and have a clear value proposition.

5. Wedding Printables 📈 GROWING

Price range: $5–$25, full suites up to $75

Wedding season drives massive Etsy traffic. Invitations, seating charts, day-of timelines, vow books — all in constant demand. The challenge: seasonal demand means peaks and valleys. The opportunity: couples will pay more for quality because the stakes feel high.

6. Notion Templates 📈 GROWING

Price range: $10–$40

Notion is growing fast and its users love custom templates. The best sellers are niche-specific: "Notion template for content creators," "freelancer client tracker in Notion," "ADHD productivity system in Notion." The audience is tech-savvy and willing to pay for a good setup they don't have to build themselves.

What's Oversaturated (Skip These Unless You Have a Twist)

  • Generic daily planners — 50,000+ listings. You need a very specific angle.
  • Generic wall art / motivational quotes — Race to $1, dominated by shops with 1,000+ reviews
  • Generic social media bundles — Too broad to stand out
  • Digital stickers without a niche — Cute but crowded

The Niche Strategy That Actually Works

Formula: [Popular product type] + [Specific audience or use case] = Lower competition, better conversion

Examples:
✓ Budget planner for nurses who work night shifts
✓ Content calendar for wedding photographers
✓ Canva media kit template for travel bloggers
✓ Weekly planner for homeschool families of 3+ kids

We built our shop, Disco Dazzler Studio, around printables and templates. Our best-converting products are the specific ones — not the "planner," but the "vacation packing checklist" or the "city scavenger hunt" that speaks to exactly one type of buyer.

How to Validate Before You Build

  1. Search your product idea on Etsy — are there listings with 100+ reviews? Good, demand exists.
  2. Look at the first page of results — can you see a gap you could fill? (Different style, different niche, better value)
  3. Check eRank or Marmalead for search volume data on your target keywords
  4. Look at the "Customers also bought" section on successful competitor listings

Don't spend three hours building something without doing this 20-minute check first. I say this from experience.
