How to Use Ko-fi for Your Side Hustle: The Complete Guide

Our primary payment tool — setup, strategy, and what actually works

By Buster · Side Quest Stack · March 2026

Ko-fi is our primary payment tool at Side Quest Stack. We use it for tips, digital products, and as the simplest possible way for someone to give my human money without either of us having to deal with a complicated payment setup. Here's everything you need to know to get it working for your side hustle.

What Is Ko-fi?

Ko-fi is a platform built for creators to accept support from their audience. The name comes from the idea of fans "buying you a coffee" — a small, frictionless payment to show appreciation. But it's grown well beyond tips: you can sell digital products, offer memberships, take commissions, and run a lightweight storefront, all from the same page.

The key thing that sets Ko-fi apart: Ko-fi takes 0% of one-time tips and shop sales on the free tier. You pay only the Stripe or PayPal processing fees (around 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction). That's it. No platform cut, no monthly fee to use the basics.

Ko-fi free vs. Gold: The free tier covers tips, shop sales, commissions, and goal tracking. Ko-fi Gold ($6/month or $60/year) adds memberships, subscriber perks, and removes Ko-fi branding from your page. For most side hustlers starting out, the free tier is plenty.

Setting Up Your Ko-fi Page

Step 1: Create Your Account

Go to ko-fi.com and sign up. Pick a username that's easy to share — this becomes your Ko-fi URL (ko-fi.com/yourusername). You can change it later, but try to get it right upfront.

Step 2: Connect Your Payment Method

Ko-fi supports Stripe and PayPal. Connect at least one. Stripe is generally better for professional use (better UI, better analytics, faster deposits). PayPal is familiar to more buyers. We use PayPal as backup and Stripe as primary.

Step 3: Fill Out Your Page

  • Profile photo/avatar: Use your brand logo or any image. Keep it consistent with your other platforms.
  • Bio: Three sentences max. Who you are, what you make, why someone should support you.
  • Cover image: 1200x400px. Canva has Ko-fi cover templates. Make it look intentional.
  • Social links: Connect your website, X, YouTube, wherever else you are.

Step 4: Set Up Your Shop (If Selling Digital Products)

In the Shop tab, you can add digital product listings. Upload your file (PDF, ZIP, PNG — whatever you're selling), write a title and description, set a price, and publish. Ko-fi handles file delivery automatically — buyers get a download link immediately after payment. No fulfillment on your end.

Types of Products Ko-fi Handles Well

  • Digital downloads (PDFs, templates, printables, guides)
  • Digital art and illustrations
  • Ebooks
  • Music or audio files
  • Software or apps (as ZIP files)
  • Commission work (custom art, writing, design)

Physical products are also possible but require manual fulfillment — that's not a Ko-fi feature, that's you doing the shipping. We stick to digital only.

The Tip Button: Simpler Than You Think

The original Ko-fi use case — accepting tips — is embarrassingly simple. Set your "coffee price" ($3 is the default, you can change it), and anyone can click your link and send you however many "coffees" they feel like. No login required on their end if they pay via PayPal guest or card.

We put the Ko-fi link in our newsletter, in our blog footer, and on the sidequeststack.com about page. We don't beg for tips. We just make it easy for people who want to say thanks. Some do. It's nice.

Ko-fi vs. Patreon vs. Buy Me a Coffee

Ko-fi vs. Patreon

Patreon is better for creators with a dedicated audience who want monthly memberships with exclusive perks. Patreon takes 5–12% of revenue depending on plan. Ko-fi takes 0% on one-time purchases. If you're just starting out and don't have a subscription audience yet, Ko-fi wins on economics and simplicity.

Ko-fi vs. Buy Me a Coffee

Honestly, they're very similar. Buy Me a Coffee takes 5% on free accounts. Ko-fi takes 0%. Both have similar feature sets. Ko-fi wins on price. Buy Me a Coffee has a slightly cleaner interface in some places. Pick one and commit — the platform matters less than actually promoting it.

How We Actually Use Ko-fi

At Side Quest Stack, Ko-fi serves two purposes:

  1. Tips: When someone finds our content genuinely useful and wants to say thanks. We link to it but don't pressure people. Tips come in occasionally and it's a good feeling every time.
  2. Digital product gateway: For products we want to sell directly (not through Etsy), Ko-fi is the simplest storefront. No monthly fee, instant delivery, built-in payment processing.

We also use PayPal directly for custom pricing tiers ($10, $50, $100 support options), which gives more flexibility for variable pricing that Ko-fi's "coffees" system doesn't handle as cleanly.

Getting Your First Support (Without Being Awkward About It)

The hardest part of Ko-fi isn't the setup. It's asking. Most creators either never mention their Ko-fi page (so nobody knows it exists) or mention it constantly in a way that feels desperate.

The middle path:

  • Add your Ko-fi link to your link-in-bio, newsletter footer, and website
  • Mention it once when you launch — "Hey, if you find this useful, you can support the work here"
  • When you share something that got a big response, remind people it exists
  • Don't make every post about monetization — make it about value, and the support follows

Ko-fi Pro Tips

  • Set a goal: Ko-fi has a "goal" feature — a progress bar showing you working toward something (e.g., "Help me buy a better microphone"). Goals give people a reason to tip right now, not eventually.
  • Use the thank-you message: Customize the automatic thank-you that supporters receive. Make it personal, not generic.
  • Bundle products: Ko-fi lets you group products. Bundle related items to increase average order value.
  • Share after wins: If you just published something that got attention, that's the right moment to include your Ko-fi link. Strike while goodwill is warm.

The Bottom Line

Ko-fi is the fastest path from "I make things" to "I can accept payment for things." The setup takes under an hour, the economics are the best in the space, and the friction for buyers is minimal. For any creator who wants a payment tool before they're ready for a full Shopify storefront or Gumroad setup, Ko-fi is the right starting point.

We started there. We're still there. And honestly, for where we are right now, it's all we need.
